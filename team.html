Project Description

Our product is a decorative toy (or souvenir) with GIX elements, made for people who know about GIX or children aged between 6 to 12. We added many different colors to the train because we believe diversity is the key to innovation, just like we GIX students have many different backgrounds. The train is equipped with cool light guide and when docked in station, it will change the pattern of the light. The toy is especially suitable for children to inspire their curiosity about technology and fabrication.

Implementation

Train Part

First, the micro-controller (Arduino Uno) was power supplied by battery. When the battery is working, there are 3 LED lights in the front of train lit up to show the train is in working state. There is a touch sensor attached to the rear of the train. When the train is docked in station and sensor is touched by the wall, the microcontroller receives a low input signal in port 5. Then the controller use port 4 and 6 to control two NeoPixel LED strips. The strips are programmable by Arduino NeoPixel library. When the train is interlocked with the station, the pattern of the two LED strips will change.

Station Part

We use one arduino to control the LED status and another arduino to control the fan. These two arduinos all should be powered by a external power supply. So in other words, the first arduino serves as a signal circuit and the second as a power circuit. The functionality of the first arduino is receiving signals from touch sensor everytime and write signal to cooresponding port to open or close power circuit through relay.  In detail, when both arduinos are powered, the yellow led will instantly light up, indicating that the station is working normally. Once the two objects are mated together, it will receive a high signal from the touch sensor and close the power circuit through a realy. So the fan will be turned on.

Software

Description: Train part

The simple code detects the low input of port 5 and then changes the pattern of two LED strips connected to part 4 and 6 by using NeoPixel library. The code was partially referred to adafruit neopixel examples but I modified the functions to make the code suitable to the size and shape the strips I use.

Description: Station part

My code implements two functions. The first is LED control. The other is Fan control. In order to detect the mate event. we use a switch to monitor its status, once it get high, meaning it's closed, the controller will  send signal to the circuit to trigger the fan. Meantime, the LED will be turned off when the objects are mated with each other.

Reflection

Jiaxuan (Jason) Xu

I was mainly responsible for making the train part. I learnt many new skills about fabrication during the final project. The first interesting thing is about light guide. For this part, we need to have a light guide from PCB to the surface wall of the object. As a result, I chose semi-transparent acrylic board to make the main body of the train so that users can see that light on the wall but couldnâ€™t see the electronic parts in the train. In order to positively interlocked with the station and create interesting reactions, I added a lock part on the rear of the train and attached a touch senor. When the sensor is touched by the station, the light guide will change the light pattern. The lights are controlled by a microcontroller (Arduino UNO), which is positively mounted by removable screws. I also made a case and a door to the battery inside the train. It was very challenging to finish to a high standard, considering the thickness of the material might vary from one and another. In order to make every part fit each seamlessly, I iterated my design in rhino many times. It is a valuable experience for me to improve my design capabilities.

Jianyu (David) Zhou

I have to say I tried my very best this time to fabricate my first interactive piece. Some design seems to be simple, while making decisions about measurement is really a tough thing, since once you make it, you will do your further design based on it. But it's very likely that there's some potential problem in the coming process, like assembeling. You will always find so many problems there that's so hard to make all of your designed components, both your physical design and your circuit, work together nicely.  Also, to properly mate with your partner's object, you two should often dicuss about the measurement to make sure there's no misunderstanding along the whole design process. Through this assignment, what teach me most is that the original design in your mind, which guide you to build your physical object, is hardest. Some interaction in your mind seems easy, while in practice, you can encounter tons of problems, some might be out of your ability to be solved.
